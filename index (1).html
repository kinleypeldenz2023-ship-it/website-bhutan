Leonardo da Vinci

Biography

Leonardo da Vinci (1452–1519) was an Italian polymath whose genius extended across painting, engineering, anatomy, architecture, music, and invention. He is widely considered the greatest Renaissance mind, blending science and art with unmatched creativity.

His notebooks contain thousands of sketches—from flying machines to anatomical studies—showing ideas centuries ahead of their time. Leonardo spent his final years in France, where he passed away in 1519 under the patronage of King Francis I.

Famous Paintings

Mona Lisa

The *Mona Lisa* (1503–1506) is Leonardo’s most iconic painting, known for her mysterious smile and soft sfumato shading.

The Last Supper

Painted between 1495–1498, this mural depicts the dramatic moment Jesus reveals that one disciple will betray him.

Virgin of the Rocks

An atmospheric and emotional painting showcasing Leonardo’s mastery of light, shading, and nature.

The Annunciation

One of Leonardo’s earliest works, painted when he was still in his 20s.

Fun Facts About Leonardo da Vinci

✔ He Was Left-Handed

Leonardo wrote most of his notes using mirror writing—from right to left—to avoid smudging ink.

✔ He Could Write and Draw at the Same Time

Leonardo was ambidextrous and could perform two different tasks with both hands simultaneously.

✔ He Loved Animals Deeply

He often bought caged birds just to set them free, showing his compassion for animals.

✔ He Was a Vegetarian

Rare for his time, Leonardo avoided eating meat because he disliked harming animals.

✔ Many Works Were Left Unfinished

Leonardo’s curiosity made him jump between projects, leaving several masterpieces incomplete.
